Common pollutant levels and their health effects

    • Last updated {{last}}
Pollutant Level Description of Pollutant
Particulate Matter (PM10)   {{pm}} µg/m³    Particulate Matter are inhalable pollutant particles with a diameter less than 10 micrometers that can enter the lungs and bloodstream, resulting in serious health issues.
Carbon Dioxide (CO2) {{co2}} ppm  Carbon dioxide emissions impact human health by displacing oxygen in the atmosphere. Breathing becomes more difficult as carbon dioxide levels rise.
Carbon Monoxide (CO) {{co}} ppm  Carbon Monoxide is a colorless and odorless gas and when inhaled at high levels can cause headache, nausea, dizziness, and vomiting. Repeated long-term exposure can lead to heart disease.
Ammonia (NH3) {{nh}} ppm    Breathing in high levels of Ammonia increases the risk of respiratory problems. Coughing and difficulty breathing are common and more serious health issues.
AQI Range Category Recommendations
0-50
 Good The level of Air Quality might have Minimal Impact.
51-100
 Satisfactory The level of Air Quality might cause minor breathing discomfort to sensitive people
101-200
 Moderate The level of Air Quality might cause breathing discomfort to the people with lung disease such as asthma and discomfort to people with heart disease, children and older adults.
201-300
 Poor The level of Air Quality might cause breathing discomfort to people on prolonged exposure and discomfort to people with heart disease with short exposure.
301-400
 Very Poor The level of Air Quality might cause respiratory illness to the people on prolonged exposure. Effect may be more pronounced in people with lung and heart diseases.
401-500
 Severe The level of Air Quality might cause respiratory effects even on healthy people and serious health impacts on people with lung/heart diseases. The health impacts may be experienced even during light physical activity.
